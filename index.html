Why We Exist

Making support easier to find for Armenian families.

Armenian Community Bridge exists because many Armenian families, students, seniors, and newcomers in Orange County and Glendale need easier access to trusted support, resources, and connection.


Our community is strong, but help is often spread across different churches, schools, organizations, social circles, and individual volunteers. For families who are new to the area, students looking for guidance, or seniors who need extra support, it can be difficult to know where to start.


We created Armenian Community Bridge to make that first step easier. Our goal is to connect Armenian community members with educational support, volunteer assistance, cultural programs, translation help, and local resources.

Get Help

Request community support.

Fill out this form if you or someone you know needs help finding tutoring, translation assistance, local resources, senior support, or newcomer guidance.


Once we receive your request, a volunteer will review it and respond as soon as possible.

Armenian Community Bridge is a volunteer community initiative, not an emergency service. For emergencies, call 911.

Volunteer

Join a student-led service network.

Volunteers help make Armenian Community Bridge possible. Students, parents, professionals, and community members can support families through tutoring, translation, outreach, events, and resource research.

Volunteer roles

  • Tutor Armenian students in school subjects
  • Help families navigate local resources
  • Assist seniors with technology or check-ins
  • Translate basic community information
  • Support cultural and service events
  • Research verified resources for the community

Founder’s Note

Built to connect, serve, and strengthen community.

I started Armenian Community Bridge to create a more organized way for Armenian families in Orange County and Glendale to find support, volunteers, and community resources.

As a student, I wanted to build something that connects generations, strengthens Armenian identity, and turns community service into practical action. This project is still growing, but its purpose is simple: to help Armenian families feel supported, seen, and connected.

— Aram Grigoryan, Founder
